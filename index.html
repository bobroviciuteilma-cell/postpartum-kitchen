Your Plan

The approach, your daily rhythm and the recovery timeline — start here.

Your Recovery Plan

Mediterranean × confinement — built for steady energy, fast repair & the least hair loss your hormones allow

Your Mediterranean base — olive oil, fish, eggs, greens, nuts, yoghurt — is the most evidence-backed anti-inflammatory recovery diet there is. The Eastern confinement layer adds warmth and blood-building, which a healing body genuinely wants. We fuse the two, lean the carbs gently lower (you lean lower-carb), and keep everything warm and nutrient-dense.

On keto while nursing — keep it keto-leaning, not strict. Your body makes milk sugar (lactose) for the baby, and very-low-carb eating in the early weeks can dip supply and, rarely, cause lactation ketoacidosis. So keep fats and protein high, but take carbs from gentle whole sources — a moderate scoop of porridge, lentils, chickpeas, berries, a date or two. Aim roughly 100–130g quality carbs a day, not 20. This is the version that makes you thrive instead of crash at week three.
On hair loss — the honest version. Postpartum shedding peaks around month 3–4 and is driven by the oestrogen drop after birth. It is partly hormonal and not fully preventable by anyone — no food stops it completely. But the severity and regrowth speed are very nutritional. Protect ferritin, protein, zinc and vitamin D and shedding stays mild, brief and fast-recovering. That is the real, achievable win this plan is built around.

Your Recovery Timeline

When to eat what — same pantry, different emphasis as you heal

Phase 1  ·  Days 1–14 — Rest & Warm

Your body: healing and bleeding, milk coming in, sluggish digestion + constipation, night sweats, hormones dropping fast.
Eat: warm, soft, cooked, easy to digest. Small frequent meals. Maximum fluids.
Lean on: bone broth daily, red date & goji tea, warm porridge/congee, khichdi & moong dal, a gentle ginger-liver soup, soft eggs, mashed sardines, cottage cheese. Start ashwagandha milk + one gond laddu a day.
Go easy on: cold & raw — big salads, cold smoothies, iced drinks; the cooling gond katira; and very gassy foods (large beans, raw cruciferous). Ease these in later.
First priority: fluids + a comfortable first bowel movement — ghee, soaked prunes/figs, flax with plenty of water, fennel-cumin water.
Stitches & comfort: soft stool is priority #1 — magnesium, plenty of fluids, soaked prunes/figs, flax (with water), and no straining. Protein, vitamin C & zinc then speed the perineal healing.

Phase 2  ·  Weeks 3–6 — Rebuild

Your body: wounds knitting, energy flickering back, milk supply settling.
Eat: keep meals warm but widen the range — warm grain & greens bowls, Mediterranean protein plates (room-temp), gradually more raw.
Double down: liver & red-meat days for ferritin, salmon/sardines for DHA, the full snack tin.
If milk dips: lean on your galactagogues — oats, moringa, fenugreek (methi), fennel.

Phase 3  ·  Week 6+ (through months 2–4) — Thrive & Defend Your Hair

Your body: 6-week check done, energy steadier — but hair shedding peaks around month 3–4.
Eat: full Mediterranean-keto — cold smoothies, fresh salads and the gond katira cooling drink are all fine now.
Don't ease off: this is exactly when ferritin, protein, zinc & vitamin D matter most. Keep liver 1–2×/wk, protein every meal, supplements going — the shedding lags the depletion, so stay ahead of it.
Don't cut calories to lose weight yet. Recovery + breastfeeding burns roughly 450–500 extra kcal a day. Keto-leaning is fine, but eat to fullness — under-eating now is the fastest way to tank milk, energy and hair. The weight settles later; nourishment comes first.
Hydration: aim for ~2.5–3 litres of warm fluids a day while nursing — broth and tea count. Keep a flask by the feeding chair.

The Meals

Breakfast, lunch, dinner and snacks — protein-forward, warm and nourishing.

Breakfast

Protein- and fat-forward, low sugar — pick one
1. Savory Turmeric Eggs ★
Build: 2–3 eggs soft-scrambled or fried in ghee or olive oil; a handful of greens wilted alongside; ½ avocado; scatter of pumpkin seeds.
Spices: turmeric + black pepper + a pinch of ajwain   |   Good for: protein, biotin, zinc, choline — the keto-Med star
2. Greek Yoghurt Recovery Bowl
Build: full-fat Greek yoghurt + berries + walnuts + 1 tbsp flax/chia + 1 scoop collagen + a drizzle of black sesame tahini; stir in ½ tsp amla. (Room-temp in the early weeks.)
Spices: cinnamon   |   Good for: hair & skin (collagen + vitamin C), omega-3, protein, gut
3. Warm Grain Porridge (moderate portion)
Build: ⅓–½ cup millet / oats / buckwheat in milk or water + ghee + 1 date; top with tahini, banana and nuts; stir moringa + amla in off the heat. Your milk-supply & steady-energy morning.
Spices: cardamom + cinnamon + dry ginger   |   Good for: milk supply, slow energy, calcium
4. Power Smoothie (your habit, lower-sugar)
Build: yoghurt, berries, coconut, collagen + protein + moringa + amla + flax; half-banana or skip it on lower-carb days. Full recipe on the Power Smoothie card.
Spices: cinnamon (+ pinch ginger)   |   Good for: fast one-handed nutrition during feeds
5. Cottage Cheese Bowl
Build: cottage cheese as the base — savory with olive oil, tomato/cucumber, seeds & herbs, or sweet with berries, flax and a drizzle of tahini. Highest-protein, lowest-sugar start; also a great before-bed repair snack.
Spices: black pepper & herbs, or cinnamon   |   Good for: slow casein protein, calcium, keto-friendly
Make it warming early on: in the first weeks lean on the eggs and porridge, take yoghurt/smoothie room-temp (not fridge-cold), and add a pinch of cinnamon or ginger. Keep berries and banana modest to stay lower-carb.

Lunch

The anchor meal — protein + iron + colour, with your fish or meat
1. Salmon + Warm Grain & Greens Bowl ★
Build: pan or oven salmon (or sardines) + ½ cup cooked barley / millet / buckwheat + wilted greens + olive oil + a black-sesame-tahini & lemon dressing + pumpkin seeds + fresh coconut.
Spices: black pepper, a little garlic   |   Good for: omega-3 (hair & mood), iron + vitamin C, complete
2. Red-Meat Iron Day (1–2× a week) ★
Build: beef or lamb + a big pile of greens + a squeeze of lemon + roasted veg or a small grain. This is your ferritin meal — the single most protective thing against hair loss.
Spices: cumin, black pepper, garlic; rosemary/thyme on the meat   |   Good for: ferritin, B12, zinc
3. Khichdi or Green-Lentil Dal + fish/meat
Build: rice or millet + moong / green lentils cooked soft in ghee; finish with moringa + lemon + a tahini drizzle; top with flaked fish or shredded meat. Gentle-gut confinement day.
Spices: cumin, ginger, turmeric, garlic, ajwain, pinch of fenugreek   |   Good for: complete protein, easy digestion, iron
4. Big Mediterranean Protein Plate (weeks 3+)
Build: chicken or fish + chickpeas + greens + olive oil + a little feta + seeds + lemon. Your "salad with fish/meat" — serve warm or room-temp in the early weeks.
Spices: oregano/thyme, black pepper   |   Good for: protein, fibre, calcium
5. Liver — confinement-style (1–2× a week) ★
Build: chicken or beef liver as a ginger-liver soup (Chinese liver-soup style), pan-seared with onions, or chicken-liver pâté on the side. Serve with greens + a squeeze of lemon. Your most concentrated recovery food.
Spices: ginger, black pepper, a little garlic   |   Good for: ferritin, vitamin A, B12, choline. 1–2×/wk, not daily — vitamin A accumulates.
Iron rule — absorb more of what you eat: use a squeeze of lemon (not amla) for the vitamin-C boost in cooked dishes — heat destroys amla's vitamin C, so keep amla for your raw morning water and smoothie. And time it right: tea, coffee and big calcium servings (dairy, cottage cheese) block iron — keep them about an hour away from your liver and red-meat meals.

Dinner

Lighter, warm, still protein-anchored — kind to the evening gut
1. Fish + Roasted Veg in Olive Oil
Build: white fish or salmon + seasonal veg roasted in olive oil or ghee. Simple, anti-inflammatory, light on digestion.
Spices: ajwain + rosemary + thyme   |   Good for: omega-3, light protein
2. Lentil / Chickpea & Greens Stew
Build: green lentils or chickpeas + greens + olive oil + a squeeze of lemon; a small grain on the side if you want it. (Or hummus made with your black sesame tahini.)
Spices: turmeric + cumin + ginger + garlic + bay   |   Good for: fibre, iron, calcium
3. Light Moong Khichdi / Broth-Barley Soup
Build: soft moong khichdi, or pearl barley simmered in your bone broth with a few red dates + a slice of ginger. The most restorative gut-rest night.
Spices: cumin + ginger + bay   |   Good for: gut-rest, warming, blood-building
4. Omelette + Greens (easy night)
Build: a 2–3 egg omelette with greens, fresh herbs and a little cheese. Five-minute complete protein when you're running on empty.
Spices: black pepper, herbs   |   Good for: fast protein, choline
Keep dinner the lightest meal. Front-load protein and carbs earlier in the day; a warm, easy-to-digest evening meal sits better while your gut recovers and supports sleep between feeds.

Postpartum Recovery Bone Broth

Makes about 3 litres  •  Simple step-by-step
WHAT YOU NEED
4 chicken feet (more if they fit)
2 chicken necks
3 litres cold water
2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
¾ tsp ginger powder
1 tsp turmeric powder
½ tsp black peppercorns
4 garlic cloves, smashed
½ tsp whole ajwain seeds
2 bay leaves
1 small sprig rosemary
1 small sprig thyme
7 red dates (jujube), each cut open a little
1 tbsp goji berries
Moringa powder — about ½ tsp per bowl (at the very end)
Salt — to taste
HOW TO MAKE IT
1
Soak. 30 min
Put the chicken feet, necks and water in a big pot. Add the apple cider vinegar. Leave it to sit with no heat for 30 minutes.
2
Boil & skim. about 15 min
Turn the heat to high and bring to a strong boil. Grey foam will rise — scoop it off with a spoon and throw it away.
3
Simmer low. 1.5–2.5 hours
Turn the heat to low (only tiny bubbles). Add ginger, turmeric, peppercorns, garlic, ajwain, bay, rosemary, thyme and red dates. Cook slowly. Every 30–60 min, scoop off foam and add a little hot water if it gets low.
4
Add goji. last 15 min only
Add the goji berries for the last 10–15 minutes only. (Longer makes them mushy and bitter.)
5
Season & strain.
Add salt slowly until it tastes good. Pour the broth through a strainer to remove the bones, dates and bits. Keep the clear liquid.
6
Moringa — in the bowl.
Do not boil the moringa. When serving, stir about ½ tsp into each bowl. The broth turns a little green — normal and good.
How to know it's done: after the broth cools in the fridge, it should look like wobbly jelly. That means it's perfect. Storing: fridge up to 4 days, or freeze in portions.
OPTIONAL — Warming "Confinement" Version (a SWAP, not an add)
For a warmer, spiced broth, change only Step 3: TAKE OUT the rosemary & thyme. PUT IN instead: 1 small cinnamon stick + 1 star anise + 2–3 whole cloves. Everything else stays the same — instead of the rosemary & thyme, not on top of them. Note: use Chinese star anise only, and keep this spiced broth for mum — not the baby.

Postpartum Teas

One daily base + boosters by job — sip warm through the day
THE DAILY BASE (on repeat)
6–8 red dates, slit open
1 tbsp goji berries (at the end)
2–3 slices fresh ginger
1 tsp honey (off the heat — never boiled)
500–750 ml water
Method: simmer dates + ginger 15–20 min. Add goji (and any steeped boosters) for the last 5 min. Take off the heat, then stir in honey. Refill the same dates for a second, lighter brew.
Keep these as their own cup:
• Chamomile — calming; an evening cup, on its own (not in the daytime mineral mix).
• Raspberry leaf — late-pregnancy birth-prep, solo, cleared with your OB. Not a postpartum blend.
BOOSTERS — ADD BY WHAT YOU NEED
Warming & digestion: 1–2 cloves · ½ cinnamon stick · 1 cardamom pod — simmer with the base
Minerals (iron/calcium): 1 nettle tea bag — steep the last 5 min, then remove (don't boil — turns bitter)
Gas, bloating & milk: ½ tsp caraway (kmynai) — your fennel stand-in; simmer with the base, or steep alone as a seed tea
Liver + milk supply: ½ tsp milk thistle seeds (margainio sėklos), lightly crushed — simmer 10 min
Mood: pinch saffron (4–6 strands), bloomed — stir in at the end
Deeper blood tonic: 4–6 dried longan — simmer with the dates
Flask shortcut: red dates + goji in a thermos of hot water — sip all day, one-handed during feeds.
Two rules: seeds (caraway, milk thistle) get lightly crushed & simmered; leaves & bags (nettle, chamomile) only steep, never boiled. Mineral base by day, chamomile in the evening.

Ashwagandha Bedtime Milk

Makes 1 mug  •  A calming nightly tonic
For AFTER birth only — not during pregnancy. If breastfeeding, it's worth checking with your doctor or lactation consultant first.
WHAT YOU NEED
1 cup (250 ml) milk (dairy, or oat/almond)
¼ tsp ashwagandha powder (start low)
1 pinch ground cardamom
1 pinch saffron, bloomed (optional — mood lift)
1 tsp ghee or coconut oil (optional — helps absorption)
1 tsp honey or maple syrup, to taste (optional)
HOW TO MAKE IT
1
Warm the milk. 2–3 min
Gently heat the milk in a small pot until steaming — not boiling.
2
Add & whisk. 2–3 min
Whisk in the ashwagandha, cardamom, bloomed saffron and ghee (if using). Whisk well so there are no lumps, then keep on low for 2–3 minutes.
3
Sweeten & sip.
Take off the heat. Stir in honey if you want it sweet. Sip warm about 30–60 minutes before bed — it's calming, so night suits it best.
Dose: start at ¼ tsp. Only move up toward ½ tsp once you know how it sits with you.
Pregnancy-safe version (for now): skip the ashwagandha and make it simply saffron + cardamom milk.
Pairs with nettle tea: nettle by day (iron & minerals), this ashwagandha milk at night (calming). Same day is fine — just keep them as two separate cups.

Gond Laddu — Highest-Nutrition Version

Makes ~18–20 balls  •  Flourless  •  After-birth food
Sourcing in Singapore: the only tricky item is the gum. Buy "gond / dink / edible gum" — the kind that puffs when fried — at Mustafa Centre or a Little India provision shop, or online (Shopee / Lazada / Amazon SG: search "edible gum" or "dink"). Not gond katira (that's the cooling one that won't fry). Everything else is at NTUC / Cold Storage / RedMart; Mustafa is cheapest for nuts, seeds & jaggery in bulk. You already have the almond flour, ajwain & moringa.
WHAT YOU NEED
BODY
½ cup edible gum (gond/dink)
½–¾ cup ghee
1–1¼ cups almond flour (flour replacement)
NUTS & SEEDS
½ cup almonds
½ cup cashews
⅓ cup walnuts (omega-3)
¼ cup pumpkin seeds (iron, zinc)
¼ cup sesame seeds (calcium)
2 tbsp ground flax or chia (omega-3)
¾ cup desiccated coconut
SWEETENER (iron-rich)
1 cup soft pitted dates (as paste) — and/or ¾ cup jaggery
SPICE & BOOSTERS
1 tsp dry ginger powder (saunth)
1 tsp cardamom powder
1 tsp ajwain (optional)
1 tsp methi/fenugreek powder (optional — milk support, bitter so keep small)
1 tbsp moringa powder (optional — iron/calcium)
Pinch (15–20 strands) saffron, bloomed (optional — makes it kesar laddu)
HOW TO MAKE IT
1
Fry the gum.
Heat ghee on medium. Add gum a little at a time — it puffs white and crisp in seconds. Scoop out, cool, crush to coarse bits.
2
Roast the almond flour. 3–4 min
In 1 tbsp ghee, roast the almond flour until fragrant and pale gold. Set aside.
3
Roast nuts, seeds & coconut.
Lightly roast the nuts, cool, and pulse to coarse rubble. Toast the coconut until just golden.
4
Make the sweetener.
Blend dates to a paste, or melt jaggery with 1 tbsp ghee into a syrup.
5
Mix while warm.
Combine crushed gum, almond flour, nuts, seeds, coconut, spices & boosters. Stir in the date paste / jaggery, bloomed saffron + enough warm ghee to bind.
6
Roll into balls.
Shape while warm. Too dry → add warm ghee or date paste. Too loose → add almond flour or coconut.
7
Cool & store.
Cool fully, then store airtight. Keeps ~3–4 weeks at room temp; freezes well. Eat 1 a day after birth.
Why it's high-nutrition: iron (dates, jaggery, pumpkin seeds, moringa), calcium (sesame, almond, gum, moringa), omega-3 (walnut, flax), plus warming energy from ghee & nuts — no empty flour filler.

Postpartum Power Smoothie

Makes 1 large glass  •  Protein, collagen, iron & vitamin C  •  No-cook
WHAT YOU NEED
BASE
1 banana
½ cup yoghurt
½ cup berries
½ cup coconut milk or water
1 scoop collagen powder
1 scoop protein powder
BOOSTERS (add as you like)
½–1 tsp moringa powder (iron, calcium, milk — start low, it's grassy)
½–1 tsp amla powder (vitamin C — absorbs the iron, builds collagen)
1–2 soft pitted dates (natural sweetness + iron)
1 tbsp ground flax or chia (omega-3 + fibre for regularity)
Pinch cinnamon or ginger (warming — for confinement balance)
HOW TO MAKE IT
1
Blend the base.
Blend banana, yoghurt, berries, coconut, collagen and protein until smooth.
2
Add your boosters.
Add any of the moringa, amla, dates, flax/chia and spice. Blend again until smooth.
3
Drink fresh.
Best straight away — the vitamin C fades the longer it stands.
The synergy: amla's vitamin C unlocks the iron (moringa) and builds the collagen — they work together. All boosters go in raw/cold to keep the vitamin C intact.
Warming tweak (confinement): with yoghurt + berries this is a cooling smoothie. In the early weeks use room-temp ingredients (not frozen/fridge-cold), add a pinch of cinnamon or ginger, and balance it with warm broth and tea through the day.

Postpartum Khichdi

Serves 3–4  •  The gentle one-pot recovery meal — soft, warm, complete protein
WHAT YOU NEED
½ cup rice (or millet)
½ cup split moong dal (yellow; softest)
4 cups water (more for softer/soupier)
1–2 tbsp ghee
1 tsp cumin seeds
1 inch ginger, grated · 1 garlic clove
½ tsp turmeric · salt
Handful greens (spinach / moringa leaves)
TO FINISH
1 tsp moringa · squeeze of lemon · extra ghee
Lunch: flaked fish or shredded meat on top
HOW TO MAKE IT
1
Rinse.
Rinse the rice + dal together until the water runs fairly clear.
2
Bloom the spices.
Heat ghee, crackle the cumin, then add ginger, garlic and turmeric for ~30 sec until fragrant.
3
Simmer. 25–30 min
Add rice, dal, water and salt. Simmer until soft and porridge-like, topping up with hot water if it thickens too much.
4
Greens.
Stir the greens in for the last 5 minutes.
5
Finish off the heat.
Stir in the moringa, a squeeze of lemon and a little extra ghee. Top with tahini; add fish or meat for lunch.
Why it's the keystone: grain + dal = complete protein, soft and easy on a healing gut. Make it soupy in the early weeks; it reheats beautifully (a great one for a helper).

Til-Gur Bites (sesame & jaggery)

Makes ~12–15  •  Calcium + iron + warmth — the classic postpartum bite
WHAT YOU NEED
1 cup black sesame seeds (til)
¾ cup jaggery, grated or powder
1–2 tbsp ghee
¼ tsp cardamom (optional)
pinch dry ginger (optional, warming)
2 tbsp chopped nuts (optional)
What it is: "til-gur" = sesame (til) + jaggery (gur) — a traditional postpartum sweet. Black sesame is one of the richest plant sources of calcium & iron; jaggery adds warmth and a little more iron. Brilliant for bones, blood and milk.
HOW TO MAKE IT
1
Toast the sesame. 3–5 min
Dry-toast the seeds in a pan on low until they pop and smell nutty. Tip out and cool slightly.
2
Melt the jaggery.
Melt the jaggery with the ghee on low heat until it just turns to a syrup — don't let it burn.

Soak the liver. 15 min

Caramelise the veg.

New to These? — Glossary

Every less-familiar ingredient in plain English — what it is & why it's in your plan
Ghee — clarified butter; the confinement cooking fat. Warming, energy-dense, helps absorb fat-soluble nutrients.
Jaggery (gur) — unrefined cane sugar; warming, with a little iron. A better sweetener than white sugar.
Amla — Indian gooseberry; one of the richest vitamin-C foods. Unlocks iron + builds collagen. ½–1 tsp powder in warm water.
Moringa — dried leaf powder; iron, calcium & milk support. Stir in off the heat (heat kills it).
Ashwagandha — calming adaptogen root. After birth only; ¼ tsp in warm milk at night. recipe →
Shatavari — a root used as a traditional milk-supply herb. After birth only (in the banana bread).
Ajwain (carom) — thyme-like seed; eases gas & digestion. Goes in broth, dal, flatbread.
Methi (fenugreek) — seed/leaf; supports milk & digestion. Bitter, so use small amounts.
Saunth — dried ginger powder; warming & digestive (more concentrated than fresh ginger).
Kesar (saffron) — flower threads; mood + warmth. Bloom a pinch in warm milk first to release it.
Caraway (kmynai) — carminative seed; your fennel stand-in for gas/bloating + milk. in the teas →
Milk thistle (margainio sėklos) — seed; liver support + traditional milk tonic. Crush & simmer in tea.
Gond / dink — edible tree gum that puffs when fried; the warming laddu gum for bones & joints. Not gond katira. recipe →
Gond katira — the cooling gum that swells in water; for heat + constipation. Always soak, never dry. recipe →
Khichdi — soft rice + lentil one-pot porridge; the gentle recovery meal. recipe →
Dal — lentils, and the spiced lentil stew made from them.
Til-gur — sesame (til) + jaggery (gur); a calcium + iron postpartum bite. recipe →
Black seed oil (kalonji) — Nigella sativa oil; anti-inflammatory / immune / milk tonic. Hold in pregnancy; ½–1 tsp/day unheated after birth.
Longan — dried sweet fruit; a blood-building tonic that deepens the red-date tea.
Red dates (jujube) & goji — Chinese blood-building tonics; iron-ish & warming, the base of your daily tea.
Tahini — sesame seed paste; calcium + healthy fat (black sesame has the most minerals).
