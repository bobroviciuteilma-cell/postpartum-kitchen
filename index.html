Your Plan

The approach, your daily rhythm and the recovery timeline — start here.

Your Recovery Plan

Mediterranean × confinement — built for steady energy, fast repair & the least hair loss your hormones allow

Your Mediterranean base — olive oil, fish, eggs, greens, nuts, yoghurt — is the most evidence-backed anti-inflammatory recovery diet there is. The Eastern confinement layer adds warmth and blood-building, which a healing body genuinely wants. We fuse the two, lean the carbs gently lower (you lean lower-carb), and keep everything warm and nutrient-dense.

On keto while nursing — keep it keto-leaning, not strict. Your body makes milk sugar (lactose) for the baby, and very-low-carb eating in the early weeks can dip supply and, rarely, cause lactation ketoacidosis. So keep fats and protein high, but take carbs from gentle whole sources — a moderate scoop of porridge, lentils, chickpeas, berries, a date or two. Aim roughly 100–130g quality carbs a day, not 20. This is the version that makes you thrive instead of crash at week three.
On hair loss — the honest version. Postpartum shedding peaks around month 3–4 and is driven by the oestrogen drop after birth. It is partly hormonal and not fully preventable by anyone — no food stops it completely. But the severity and regrowth speed are very nutritional. Protect ferritin, protein, zinc and vitamin D and shedding stays mild, brief and fast-recovering. That is the real, achievable win this plan is built around.

Your Recovery Timeline

When to eat what — same pantry, different emphasis as you heal

Phase 1  ·  Days 1–14 — Rest & Warm

Your body: healing and bleeding, milk coming in, sluggish digestion + constipation, night sweats, hormones dropping fast.
Eat: warm, soft, cooked, easy to digest. Small frequent meals. Maximum fluids.
Lean on: bone broth daily, red date & goji tea, warm porridge/congee, khichdi & moong dal, a gentle ginger-liver soup, soft eggs, mashed sardines, cottage cheese. Start ashwagandha milk + one gond laddu a day.
Go easy on: cold & raw — big salads, cold smoothies, iced drinks; the cooling gond katira; and very gassy foods (large beans, raw cruciferous). Ease these in later.
First priority: fluids + a comfortable first bowel movement — ghee, soaked prunes/figs, flax with plenty of water, fennel-cumin water.
Stitches & comfort: soft stool is priority #1 — magnesium, plenty of fluids, soaked prunes/figs, flax (with water), and no straining. Protein, vitamin C & zinc then speed the perineal healing.

Phase 2  ·  Weeks 3–6 — Rebuild

Your body: wounds knitting, energy flickering back, milk supply settling.
Eat: keep meals warm but widen the range — warm grain & greens bowls, Mediterranean protein plates (room-temp), gradually more raw.
Double down: liver & red-meat days for ferritin, salmon/sardines for DHA, the full snack tin.
If milk dips: lean on your galactagogues — oats, moringa, fenugreek (methi), fennel.

Phase 3  ·  Week 6+ (through months 2–4) — Thrive & Defend Your Hair

Your body: 6-week check done, energy steadier — but hair shedding peaks around month 3–4.
Eat: full Mediterranean-keto — cold smoothies, fresh salads and the gond katira cooling drink are all fine now.
Don't ease off: this is exactly when ferritin, protein, zinc & vitamin D matter most. Keep liver 1–2×/wk, protein every meal, supplements going — the shedding lags the depletion, so stay ahead of it.
Don't cut calories to lose weight yet. Recovery + breastfeeding burns roughly 450–500 extra kcal a day. Keto-leaning is fine, but eat to fullness — under-eating now is the fastest way to tank milk, energy and hair. The weight settles later; nourishment comes first.
Hydration: aim for ~2.5–3 litres of warm fluids a day while nursing — broth and tea count. Keep a flask by the feeding chair.

Breakfast

Spices: black pepper & herbs, or cinnamon   |   Good for: slow casein protein, calcium, keto-friendly

Lunch

The anchor meal — protein + iron + colour, with your fish or meat

Spices: cumin, black pepper, garlic; rosemary/thyme on the meat   |   Good for: ferritin, B12, zinc
3. Khichdi or Green-Lentil Dal + fish/meat
Build: rice or millet + moong / green lentils cooked soft in ghee; finish with moringa + lemon + a tahini drizzle; top with flaked fish or shredded meat. Gentle-gut confinement day.

4. Big Mediterranean Protein Plate (weeks 3+)
Build: chicken or fish + chickpeas + greens + olive oil + a little feta + seeds + lemon. Your "salad with fish/meat" — serve warm or room-temp in the early weeks.
Spices: oregano/thyme, black pepper   |   Good for: protein, fibre, calcium
5. Liver — confinement-style (1–2× a week) ★
Build: chicken or beef liver as a ginger-liver soup (Chinese liver-soup style), pan-seared with onions, or chicken-liver pâté on the side. Serve with greens + a squeeze of lemon. Your most concentrated recovery food.
Spices: ginger, black pepper, a little garlic   |   Good for: ferritin, vitamin A, B12, choline. 1–2×/wk, not daily — vitamin A accumulates.
Iron rule — absorb more of what you eat: use a squeeze of lemon (not amla) for the vitamin-C boost in cooked dishes — heat destroys amla's vitamin C, so keep amla for your raw morning water and smoothie. Chicken Bone Broth

Deep iron & collagen — sip a warm cup a day  •  makes ~3 L
WHAT YOU NEED conversions →
4 chicken feet (more if they fit)
2 chicken necks
3 litres cold water
2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
¾ tsp ginger powder
1 tsp turmeric powder
½ tsp black peppercorns
4 garlic cloves, smashed
½ tsp whole ajwain seeds
2 bay leaves
1 sprig rosemary · 1 sprig thyme
Salt — to taste
HOW TO MAKE IT
1
Soak. 30 min
Chicken feet, necks and water in a big pot with the cider vinegar. Sit, no heat, 30 min.
2
Boil & skim. ~15 min
Boil hard; scoop off and discard the grey foam.
3
Simmer low — this one matters
Keep it at a bare simmer for 1.5–2.5 hrs — only a few lazy bubbles should break the surface, never a hard, rolling boil. A high flame makes the broth cloudy and greasy and breaks down the collagen. Add ginger, turmeric, peppercorns, garlic, ajwain, bay, rosemary and thyme; skim and top up with hot water now and then.
4
Season, strain & serve.
Salt to taste; strain off the bones and bits.
PP Gold Version
Turn it into the warming confinement broth — just add:
7 red dates, split · with the spices (step 3)
1 tbsp goji · last 15 min only (longer turns them bitter)
½ tsp moringa · per bowl, off the heat (never boil)
For extra warmth, swap the rosemary & thyme for 1 cinnamon stick + 1 star anise + 2–3 cloves · the spiced version is for mum, not baby
Good to know — set like jelly when chilled, that's perfect. Sip a warm cup daily or use as a soup base. Stirred-in moringa turns the broth a little green — normal and good. Keeps 4 days in the fridge, or freezes in portions.
Reheat by the cup, not the pot — ladle out just what you want and warm that one portion (a mug in the microwave, or a small pan). Bringing the whole batch back to the boil again and again dulls the flavour, clouds it, and shortens how long it keeps.
For little ones Lovely from ~6 months as a cooking liquid — use it for baby's congee, purées or to soften grains. Leave the salt out for under-1s (season your own bowl after). The ginger and turmeric are gentle in these small amounts.

Meat Stock

The gut-healing base — raw meaty bones, a short simmer. Gentle “heal & seal” nourishment for you and baby  •  2–3 hrs
Meat stock vs bone broth
Meat stock is made from a raw joint or bone with the meat still attached, simmered a short time (2–3 hrs). That makes it gentler and easier to digest than long-cooked bone broth — it’s the classic “heal & seal” gut food, and a lovely first base in the tender early weeks. (Bone broth = mostly bones, simmered long for deep minerals.)
WHAT YOU NEED conversions →
Raw meaty bones — a joint or bone with meat attached (see "choose your meat")
+ feet or necks every time — the most collagen
Filtered water — 1 quart per 1 lb meaty bones
Sea salt, peppercorns & a few sage leaves (at the start)
Nice to add — carrots, onion, fresh herbs, garlic
Leave out — broccoli, cauliflower, kale (they overtake the flavour)
HOW TO MAKE IT
1
Start raw & cold.
Raw meaty bones and filtered water in a heavy-bottomed pot — 1 quart water per 1 lb bones. Add the sea salt, peppercorns and sage now.
2
Boil briefly. 5–10 min
Bring to a boil for 5–10 minutes.
3
Cover & simmer low. 2–3 hrs
Drop to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook until the meat falls off the bone.
4
Strain & cool.
Pour through a fine-mesh strainer into a metal or glass bowl — never hot liquid in plastic. Fridge or freeze once cooled.
Choose your meat
All gut-healing — tap one for the cut & ratio.
~4 qt · 2 hrs

Chicken

1 whole chicken (~4 lb) + ~4 qt filtered water (about 1:1 meat-to-water). Add chicken feet or necks for collagen. Boil 5–10 min, cover and simmer 2 hrs, then strain. Whole bird/carcass, feet, necks & bones all work.

1 qt / lb · 2–3 hrs

Beef

A joint or bone with meat attached — beef shank, knuckle, or rib & shank. Use 1 quart water per 1 lb. Boil briefly, then simmer 2–3 hrs. The richest, most collagen-heavy gut-healing stock.

1 qt · 2–3 hrs

Lamb

1 lb lamb neck or shank + 1 qt filtered water. Boil 5–10 min, then simmer covered 2–3 hrs until the meat falls off the bone.

Boost your cup — add-ins
Stir extra nourishment into a warm cup:

• Egg yolk — choline and a creamy cup (start with 2).
• Butter or ghee — fat-soluble vitamins; nourishes the gut wall.
• Grated garlic — immune & digestive support.
• Green onion — gentle phytonutrients.
• A spoon of yogurt or sauerkraut juice — probiotics & vitamin C; stir in once it has cooled a little, so the live cultures survive.
Didn’t gel? — quick fixes
A good stock sets like soft jelly when cold. If yours stays watery:

• Ratio — 1 lb meaty bones to 1 quart water; less water, more gel.
• Use the gelatin-rich bits — feet, necks, knuckles, backs.
• Gentle heat — a soft, bubbly simmer (movement underneath, calm on top), lid on.
• Cook till it lets go — it’s ready when the meat falls off the bone.
• Skip lean cuts — breast meat and steaks won’t gel.
• Hard to hold a simmer? Move the covered pot to a low oven (250°F / 120°C).
• Cloudy? Skim the foam more in the first 15 minutes.
Make a second batch — resteep
Don’t toss the bones after one cook — get a second, lighter batch from them. Cover them with 2–3 more quarts of fresh water, add another tbsp of sea salt, and simmer 2–3 hours. Less rich than the first, but still nourishing — twice the stock from the same bones. (The first batch’s veggies are spent; eat or toss them and add fresh.)
Why it heals — gelatin & collagen
Meat stock is rich in collagen, elastin, proteoglycans, glycoproteins and hyaluronic acid — the molecules that build connective tissue (most of your body). The collagen from feet, necks and joints is exactly what supports your own repair postpartum. Because it’s simmered briefly, it’s gentler and easier to digest than long-cooked bone broth — the “heal & seal” food at the heart of the GAPS gut-healing approach (Dr. Natasha Campbell-McBride). Food, not medicine.
For little ones — starting baby
Meat stock helps heal & seal baby’s naturally semi-permeable gut — the perfect foundation for you both.

• Plain first: just your cut of meat + filtered water. Add sea salt only after ~10 weeks of solids; veggies one at a time, after salt.
• Start: when baby’s ready for solids, give 1–2 tsp before nursing for one whole week.
• Feed: bottle, cup, spoon, or cold & jelly. No hot liquid in plastic.
• If baby resists: keep offering — babies can dislike a flavour up to the 8th try. Stay calm & patient.
How much — sip it like tea: about 4–6 cups a day for you, 2–4 for a little one. Any time, and especially when you feel run-down.
Good to know — it should set like soft jelly when chilled; that wobble is the collagen. Sip it, or use it as the base for soups, pho, congee and porridge. Strain into glass (hot stock sterilises the jar — keeps 7–10 days); or freeze in portions, leaving room to expand.

Meat Stock Latte

A warm, frothy, egg-enriched cup — sip it when you’re run-down or feel a cold coming on  •  1 mug  •  5 min
Built on Meat Stock → make this first A lovely way to drink your stock — bone broth works too.
WHAT YOU NEED conversions →
2 cups warm meat stock (warm, not boiling)
2 egg yolks (yolks only — see the note)
1 good spoonful ghee (garlic & rosemary is lovely)
HOW TO MAKE IT
1
Warm the stock.
Gently warm 2 cups of stock — warm, not boiling (boiling would scramble the yolks).
2
Into the blender.
Pour it into a blender, or a jar if you’re using a stick blender.
3
Add ghee & yolks.
A good spoonful of ghee, and the two egg yolks only (separate off the whites).
4
Blend & sip. ~30 sec
Whizz until smooth and frothy, pour into your favourite mug. Frothtastic.
Best time to make it — when a cough or sore throat shows up, or any day you’re sick or just doubling down. Quick comfort in a mug.
A note on raw yolks
The warm-not-boiling stock leaves the yolks essentially raw. Use the freshest, best-quality (ideally pasteurised) eggs you can. If you’re avoiding raw egg, stir the latte over low heat until it thickens slightly like a thin custard, or simply leave the yolks out.
For little ones A gentle, frothy way to get stock into a toddler too — but for babies and little ones, make the egg fully cooked (warm it through until set) rather than raw, or just skip the yolk.

Postpartum Teas

One daily base + boosters by job — sip warm through the day
THE DAILY BASE (on repeat)
6–8 red dates, slit open
1 tbsp goji berries (at the end)
2–3 slices fresh ginger
1 tsp honey (off the heat — never boiled)
500–750 ml water
Method: simmer dates + ginger 15–20 min. Add goji (and any steeped boosters) for the last 5 min. Take off the heat, then stir in honey. Refill the same dates for a second, lighter brew.
Keep these as their own cup:
• Chamomile — calming; an evening cup, on its own (not in the daytime mineral mix).
• Raspberry leaf — late-pregnancy birth-prep, solo, cleared with your OB. Not a postpartum blend.
BOOSTERS — ADD BY WHAT YOU NEED
Warming & digestion: 1–2 cloves · ½ cinnamon stick · 1 cardamom pod — simmer with the base
Minerals (iron/calcium): 1 nettle tea bag — steep the last 5 min, then remove (don't boil — turns bitter)
Gas, bloating & milk: ½ tsp caraway (kmynai) — your fennel stand-in; simmer with the base, or steep alone as a seed tea
Liver + milk supply: ½ tsp milk thistle seeds (margainio sėklos), lightly crushed — simmer 10 min
Mood: pinch saffron (4–6 strands), bloomed — stir in at the end
Deeper blood tonic: 4–6 dried longan — simmer with the dates
Flask shortcut: red dates + goji in a thermos of hot water — sip all day, one-handed during feeds.
Two rules: seeds (caraway, milk thistle) get lightly crushed & simmered; leaves & bags (nettle, chamomile) only steep, never boiled. Mineral base by day, chamomile in the evening.

Calming Bedtime Milk (Ashwagandha)

Makes 1 mug  •  A calming nightly tonic

Nut & Gum Energy Balls (Gond Laddu)

The Healing Guide

The why behind the food — tap any to learn more. Food, not medicine.
What is leaky gut?when the gut lining gets too leaky
Your gut wall is meant to be a tight barrier. When the junctions between its cells loosen, undigested food, toxins and proteins slip into the bloodstream — confusing the immune system and leaving you inflamed and poorly nourished. Modern life is hard on it: sugar, processed food, stress and toxins all play a part.
What is GAPS?the gut-healing approach behind meat stock
GAPS — Gut and Psychology Syndrome — is a nutritional approach from Dr. Natasha Campbell-McBride. The idea: heal the gut first, and the rest of the body can follow. Meat stock is its foundational food, sipped daily to soothe and rebuild the gut lining. (Worth discussing any big dietary change with your own practitioner.)
Heal & seal — why stock helpswhat makes stock so restorative
A gentle meat stock is rich in gelatin, collagen and easy-to-digest amino acids — the building blocks your gut lining uses to repair itself. Because it’s simmered briefly, it’s lighter than long-cooked bone broth and kinder to a tender, tired system. That’s why it’s the first food in gut healing. the meat stock recipe →
Best salts for cookingskip table salt; reach for mineral salt
Use an unrefined mineral salt — it brings trace minerals, not just sodium.

• Celtic sea salt — the highest mineral content; a lovely everyday choice.
• Redmond Real Salt — mid-range, from an ancient sea bed.
• Pink Himalayan — lighter mineral content, but fine.
Avoid processed table salt.
Herbs for your stockgentle additions that aid digestion
All optional, all kind to digestion:

• Peppercorns — help you absorb more.
• Ginger — settles nausea.
• Sage — soothing and antioxidant.
• Rosemary — antimicrobial.
• Thyme — vitamin C.
• Parsley — gentle drainage.
